## B1-1 WINGS
Roll 2D per shell hit.

| ROLL | AREA HIT | EFFECT |
|------|----------|--------|
| 2-3 | Wing Root | 1 wing root hit (a) |
| 4 | Wing Flap | Roll 1D: 1-3 = Flap inoperable (b); 4-6 = No effect. |
| 5 | Aileron | Roll 1D: 1-3 = Aileron inoperable (b); 4-6 = No effect. |
| 6-8 | Superficial Damage | No effect. |
| 9 | Engines | Roll 1D: If port wing—1-3 = #1 engine; 4-6 = #2 engine. If starboard wing—1-3 = #3 engine; 4-6 = #4 engine. Roll 1D again: 1,2 = Superficial Damage; 3,4 = Engine out (c); 5 = Runaway engine (d); 6 = Oil tank hit (e). |
| 10 | Fuel Tank | Roll 1D: 1-3 = Outboard tank; 4-6 = Inboard tank. Roll 1D again: 1,2 = Fire (f); 3,4 = Leakage (g); 5,6 = Self-seal, no effect. |
| 11 | Wing Root | See effect for roll of 2 or 3 above. |
| 12 | Landing Gear | Roll 1D: 1-3 = Brake out (h); 4-6 = Landing gear inoperable (i). |

Notes:
a) When a wing root has accumulated 5 hits over the course of a mission, the wing rips off and the crew must immediately bailout according to Table G-7.
b) If both the port and starboard wing flaps or ailerons are inoperable, the landing roll on Table G-9 or G-10 is −1.
c) Roll 1D: 1-5 = Prop feathered; 6 = Prop not feathered, B-17 must spend 2 turns per zone due to added drag, and is out of formation (See rules sections Engines Out and Out of Formation.)
d) Roll 1D: 1-5 = Prop feathered and engine out; 6 = Prop not feathered, engine out of control—crew must immediately bailout according to Table G-6.
e) Roll 1D: 1-2 = Engine fire (Each engine has 2 extinguisher charges per mission. Roll 1D: 1-4 = Fire out; 5,6 = Fire continues—try again. If second try fails, crew must immediately bailout according to Table G-6.); 3-4 = Leakage (Roll 1D: 1,2 = Engine must be shut off after 1 more zone of travel; 3,4 = Shut off engine after 2 more zones; 5,6 = Turn off engine after 3 more zones.); 5-6 = Self-seal, no effect.
f) Roll 1D: 1-5 = Fire continues, bailout, Table G-6; 6 = Explosion, bailout, Table G-7.
g) Roll 1D: (B-17 may travel the following number of zones, then bailout, Table G-6, or crash land, Table G-9 or G-10.) 1,2 = 2 zones; 3,4 = 5 zones; 5,6 = 6 zones. If this is the second separate tank hit, B-17 may go only 2 more zones. If this is the third separate tank hit, B-17 may only go 1 more zone.
h) Landing roll on Table G-9 −1.
i) If the landing gear on either or both sides is inoperable, landing roll on Table G-9 is −3.

## B1-3 HAND HELD EXTINGUISHERS
Roll 1D per attempt to extinguish fire.

| ROLL | EFFECT |
|------|--------|
| 1-4 | Fire out |
| 5,6 | Fire continues, try again (3 try limit) |

There are 3 portable fire extinguishers in the B-17, represented by individual counters placed on the Crew Placement Chart. Each may be sprayed once and then it's empty. A crewman may not operate a gun during an attack in which he is fighting a fire. A crewman may immediately attempt 3 times to put out a fire (i.e., use up 3 extinguishers). If the fire is not out after the 3rd try, the crew must immediately bailout, Table G-6. Used extinguishers are removed from play.

## B1-4 WOUNDS
Roll 1D per affected crew member.

| ROLL | EFFECT |
|------|--------|
| 1-3 | Light Wound: Crewman may continue duties unimpaired. (a) |
| 4,5 | Serious Wound: Crewman may not continue duties, may not bailout. (b) |
| 6 | KIA: Crewman killed in action. |

Notes:
a) A 2nd light wound results in: Gunners may only hit fighters with a roll of 6; Bombardier loses his 11-25th mission bonus and the Bomb Run roll on Table O-6 is −1; Pilot and Co-Pilot lose their 11-25th mission bonus for landing (but if either hasn't taken 2 light wounds he may land the plane using his bonus); Bailout for this crewman on Table G-6 is −1. 3 light wounds = a serious wound. 4 light wounds = KIA. Light wound + serious wound = KIA.
b) After landing roll 1D for each seriously wounded crewman for survival: 1 = Rapid recovery, may fly on next mission; 2-5 = Recovery, but may not fly any more missions; 6 = Wounds fatal, crewman dies.

## G-10 LANDING IN WATER
Roll 2D.

| ROLL | EFFECT |
|------|--------|
| 2-3 | Crew lost |
| 4-12 | Crew rescued |

Notes:
a) A roll of 12 is always "crew safe" regardless of negative modifiers in effect.
b) If Pilot and/or Co-Pilot are on 11-25th mission, die roll is +1.
c) If landing in Germany or Netherlands, entire crew is captured.
d) If landing in France or Belgium, for each surviving crewman roll 1D: 1-5 = Captured; 6 = rescued by Underground and returned to England in time for next mission. (Seriously wounded crewmen are automatically captured.)
e) If roll is 0 or less and bombs are still aboard, roll 1D: 1-5 = No effect; 6 = Explosion, B-17 and all remaining crewmen destroyed.
f) If the Pilot and Co-Pilot are dead or seriously wounded and another crewman is attempting to land the B-17, landing roll is −11.
g) If landing in water with the radio out, landing roll is −6, if out of formation.
h) If landing with only 1 engine operating, landing roll is −3.
i) If landing with no engines operating, landing roll is −7 (if landing in water, −4).
j) If landing on land not in England, landing roll is −3.

## ERRATA AND CLARIFICATIONS (Page 36)

1. Table B1-3 should read "Roll 1D per attempt to extinguish fire."
2. Although there are six fire extinguisher counters provided, only five should be in play.
3. Table M-2, under Note (a), "FE 190" should read "FW 190". Also add: 2 or more FCA = FBOA; 2 or more FBOA = Destroyed; FCA + FBOA = FBOA.
4. Fighter attack die roll modifiers are cumulative for additional FCA and FBOA hits, except that a roll of "6" always hits. A fighter destroyed (even by accumulation of hits) does not get to attack.
5. Recovery from frostbite is correct as shown in Note (b) to Table B1-5, not as shown in Rules Section 11.0.
6. On Table G-3, both Brest and St. Nazaire should be marked with an asterisk for increased flak occurrence.
7. In Rules Section 18.0, Random Events, the Random Events Table is incorrectly listed as "G-11"; it should be "B-7".
8. The notes to Table G-7 apply to Tables G-6 and G-8 as well.
9. If the Navigator is KIA or Seriously Wounded, Table P-1 Note (b) applies.
10. On Table B-5, 6 O'Clock High, Roll "11", "Walking Hits/Fuselage" should be Type (a).
11. In the rules on page 11, Section 6.4 should read "3-1 = 2 = One Wave."
12. There are no landing dice roll modifiers for having 1 or 2 engines out.
13. The B-17 takes a hit on Table P-1 (Nose Compartment), #10, rolls a second die "4-6" = Bomb Run −3. If this damage was done by flak the Bomb Run roll is now −4 (−3 for the damage, and −1 for being hit by flak during the Bomb Run).
14. If the "No Attackers" result is rolled on Table B-3, then the additional fighters which attack the lead and tail position bombers do not appear.
15. Spray fire is directed at a specific clock position (6 O'Clock High), not at individual attacking fighters. If more than one fighter attacks from this position (during a successive attack) roll once on Table M-5 for each.
16. If a B-17 takes a fuel tank hit which doesn't leave it enough fuel to bomb the target and return, the B-17 may abort the mission and still count it towards the 25 mission campaign.
17. A B-17 is never required to spend more than 2 Turns/Zone.
18. Table B1-1, Roll "10", Case (g) should say "4, 5, or 6 Turns" left, not Zones.
19. If a fuel tank which is already leaking is hit again, roll for damage normally (either fire, leak or self seal). The previous hit has no effect.
20. If an engine fire is put out by extinguishers, the engine is out and feathered.
21. If the Pilot Compartment Windshield is knocked out (Table P-2, #11), the heat is out for this compartment (Pilot and Co-Pilot heat out).
22. Rouen is in Zone 4, not in Zone 5 as shown on Chart G-11, Flight Log Gazetteer.
23. A B-17 can be irreparably damaged only by a flak BIP or crash landing.
24. Crew members may voluntarily bail out over England (Table G-6) before attempting a dangerous landing.
25. Vegesack is a U-Boat type target with increased flak (+1 to die roll on Table O-2).
26. When a B-17 lands in England with no landing gear, an additional roll is made for irreparable damage. If the plane does not crash, roll 1D: 1-3 = B-17 is reparable; 4-6 = B-17 irreparably damaged.
27. The Radio Operator must be in the Radio Room to consider the radio operational and avoid the negative modifier for landing.
28. The altitude drop required by the loss of oxygen does not occur during the turn in which the damage takes place. Begin the next turn at 10,000.
29. When a result of "2" is rolled on Table B-7 Random Events, roll for feathering the affected engine according to Note (c) Table B1-1.
30. Any guns jammed by extreme cold (from a result of "10" rolled on Table B-7 Random Events) are automatically unjammed if the B-17 drops to 10,000 feet.
31. A B-17 which must make a mandatory crash landing its next turn must resolve fighter attacks normally before landing.
32. When entering the Designated Target Zone roll for weather before determining Fighter Cover (Table G-5) or attacking enemy fighter waves (Table B-2).
33. The Ace bonus does apply to Tail Guns passing shots (see 9.2).
34. On Table G-8, Bailout Over Water, Note (b) applies only if the B-17 is out of formation when bailout occurs.
35. On Chart G-11, Flight Log Gazetteer, Note (a) applies to Table B-2 also.
